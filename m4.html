  • two types of coffee tree

  • coffee production is labor intensive

    • cherries don't ripen at the same time, so you'll be picking from the same tree over and over
    • mechanical harvesting is an option, but unripe coffee cherries are collected and therefore wasted
    • 100kg of cherries -> 12-20 kg of beans
    • stripping / mechanically harvested coffee needs to be sorted
    • manual sorting is slow, so water is used
      • good cherries sink
    • pulp is removed from seeds
      • you can push them through a screen if you want
    • then mucilage (remaining pit) has to be removed
      • microorganisms go to town on this shit
    • now the beans are ~60% moisture, we need 11-15%
      • drying patios: literally leave them on a slab in the sun
      • drying tables: basically hammocks of mesh that get spun
    • last moisture is removed by oven
      • ovens are like clothes dryers, tumble drying the beans
      • stage 1: slow dry
      • stage 2: 400F, beans swell to twice their size, first crack
      • stage 3, 428F, beans are now a medium brown
      • stage 4: 437ishF, final pop, oily sheen
        • amt of time here determines light / med / dark roast
      • roast the fuck out of coffee to mask imperfections
