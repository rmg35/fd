  • we overcook pork, but not beef!

  • sus scrofa == scientific name for pig

  • pigs were first tamed in 10,000BC

    • domesticated in china ~4900BC
    • europe ~1500BC

  • big bill (jackson, tn, us) was the largest pig in the world

  • pigs aren't native to NA

    • columbus brought them at queen isabella's request
    • de soto brought pigs before, and released them into the wild

  • iowa has the most pigs in the US

    • minnesota and north carolina trail it

  • beef and pork rule in terms of production, but chicken leads in consumption

  • us consumes less pork than other countries, but more beef and poultry

  • pigs were originally raised for lard

    • fed grain diets to pack on the pounds
    • pigs raised for bacon are given a more varied diet, and grow more lean

  • before WW2, lard was the shit for frying stuff in

    • WW2 happens, US needs lard for explosives, no more lard for the people
    • they turn to vegetable oil instead, and never turn back
    • less call for lard, less call for lard pigs
    • more call for leaner meat pigs

  • leaner cuts of pork actually have less calories than lean cuts of chicken or beef

  • pigs are the fourth most intelligent animals

    • they're also relatively competent (and destructive) pets

  • OG pigs were raised on small farms with little management

    • allowed to forage in the fields
    • fed scraps and old, often rotten, food
    • pigs don't sweat, and so regulated their body temperature using mud

  • modern pig farms are indoors

    • no mud for pigs, they get A/C instead
    • floors designed to remove waste
    • robot wash carts to spray water at waste

  • whole lotta pig slander

    • pouring coke on a slab of pork causes worms to crawl out

  • pigs are used to hunt truffles (fungi)

    • they can sniff them out

  • all raw meat has bacteria on its surface

    • pork tends to carry trichinella spiralis, a worm
    • that worm causes trichinosis
    • larvae form cysts in the muscles, someone eats the cysts, they got worms, owned

  • feed a pig infected junk, they'll get the worms

    • eat the uncooked pig, you get them too
    • these days, we don't let pigs get sick like that
    • most cases in the US are from undercooked game meats
    • you can kill the worms with heat, hence why cooking works

  • pork is sterile on the inside

    • pork chops at 145F, ground meat at 160F
